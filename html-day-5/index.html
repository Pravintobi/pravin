Kazi Nazrul Islam

The iconic poet of Bangladesh

The Rebel Poet

Kazi Nazrul Islam, a prominent Bengali poet, writer, journalist, and musician, is the national poet of Bangladesh. He produced a vast collection of works encompassing poetry, music, novels, stories, and messages that explored themes of equality, justice, anti-imperialism, humanity, and rebellion against oppression, along with religious devotion.



Acclaimed Writer

Nazrul composed nearly 4,000 songs, widely known as Nazrul Geeti. However, at the age of 43, he suffered from an illness that led to memory and voice loss. He was later diagnosed with Pick's disease, which significantly affected his health and forced him into isolation. Nazrul spent several years in Ranchi's psychiatric hospital. In 1972, his family moved him to Bangladesh at the invitation of the government. He passed away on 29 August 1976.
